## Media

My work on detecting racial avoidance in New York City was covered in the [Journalist's Resource](https://journalistsresource.org/race-and-gender/racial-avoidance-new-york-city/).

My work on exposure to inequality in South Africa was mentioned in [Bloomberg Opinion](https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2019-07-02/u-s-middle-class-isn-t-just-defined-by-income-but-outlook).

## Policy and Presentations

Read our [Scholars Strategy Network brief](https://scholars.org/brief/how-citizen-attachment-neighborhoods-helps-improve-municipal-services-and-public-spaces) about how citizen attachment to neighborhoods helps improve municipal services and public spaces.

[Here are slides](https://www.dropbox.com/s/6ms49h2wpdu4ttu/segregation_dekadtsands_FINALVERSION.pdf?dl=0) for my study on the effects of segregation on voting behavior in South Africa, presented at the [Harvard Meeting on Political Geography in 2015](http://projects.iq.harvard.edu/files/political_geography/files/political-geography-conference_3.pdf).

[Here are slides](https://www.dropbox.com/s/2byfbchei2dtt8p/spatialprez.pdf?dl=0) that provide an instructive overview to handling spatial data in R, presented at the UCLA Political Methodology Workshop in 2014.

I served as a consultant on the National Academy of Sciences/National Research Council report, [Using Science as Evidence in Public Policy](https://www.nap.edu/catalog/13460/using-science-as-evidence-in-public-policy). The project details the importance of scientific evidence in public policy-making, and outlines a new research agenda in this area.
